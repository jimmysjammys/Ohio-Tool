☭ Marxist Library — Theoretical Foundations
Primary texts for materialist analysis of state power, capital, and class — directly applicable to Ohio legislative commentary. All links open Marxists Internet Archive (marxists.org), the largest free digital library of socialist literature.
Labor & Class Struggle
Capital, Volume I — Marx
The commodity, surplus value, the working day, primitive accumulation. The foundational text for understanding how capital extracts value from labor — directly applicable to Ohio wage suppression and right-to-work legislation.
Marx · 1867
Wage Labour and Capital — Marx
Concise explanation of the wage relation, competition between workers, and capital accumulation. Accessible entry point — maps directly onto Ohio labor law debates and union suppression.
Marx · 1847
Value, Price and Profit — Marx
Marx's clearest explanation of surplus value for a working-class audience. Short, readable, and devastating — useful for framing Ohio Chamber of Commerce-backed legislation.
Marx · 1865
The Communist Manifesto — Marx & Engels
Class struggle as the motor of history; the bourgeois state as an executive committee. The essential framing document — the Ohio Republican supermajority is a case study in this analysis.
Marx & Engels · 1848
State Power & Bourgeois Democracy
The State and Revolution — Lenin
The state as an instrument of class rule, not a neutral arbiter. Essential for cutting through procedural arguments about Ohio redistricting, legislative process, and "democracy" rhetoric from Republicans.
Lenin · 1917
The Civil War in France — Marx
Marx on the Paris Commune — state power, bourgeois repression, and the difference between parliamentary and popular democracy. Frames questions about Ohio ballot initiative suppression.
Marx · 1871
The Origin of the Family, Private Property and the State — Engels
How the state arose to protect private property and class interests. Relevant to Ohio property tax legislation, inheritance law, and the ideological function of "family values" legislation.
Engels · 1884
Selections from the Prison Notebooks — Gramsci
Hegemony, civil society, and the "war of position." Explains how Republican ideological dominance in Ohio is reproduced through media, education, and cultural institutions — not just legislation.
Gramsci · 1929–35
Imperialism & Military Spending
Imperialism, the Highest Stage of Capitalism — Lenin
Finance capital, monopoly, and the export of capital as the basis of modern imperialism. The theoretical backbone for critiquing the defense budget, Pentagon contracting, and Ohio's military-industrial presence.
Lenin · 1916
The Accumulation of Capital — Luxemburg
Militarism as a mechanism of capital accumulation. Directly applicable to the H.R. 1 defense spending mandate and the contradiction between military bloat and domestic austerity.
Luxemburg · 1913
Capital Ch. 25: The General Law of Capitalist Accumulation — Marx
The reserve army of labor, relative surplus population, and immiseration. Framework for understanding military recruitment pipelines from Ohio's working-class and rural communities.
Marx · 1867
Party Organization & Political Strategy
What Is to Be Done? — Lenin
Vanguard organization, trade union consciousness vs. socialist consciousness, and the role of political agitation. Relevant to questions about building effective Ohio political commentary and movement infrastructure.
Lenin · 1902
The 18th Brumaire of Louis Bonaparte — Marx
"History repeats itself, first as tragedy, then as farce." Marx on bonapartism, lumpenproletariat politics, and how reactionary movements use democratic forms. Essential for analyzing Trumpism and Ohio's MAGA apparatus.
Marx · 1852
The Russian Revolution — Luxemburg
Luxemburg's internal critique of Bolshevik methods — on democracy, freedom of the press, and socialist organization. A counterweight for dialectical thinking about ends and means in political strategy.
Luxemburg · 1918
Critique of the Gotha Programme — Marx
Marx's sharpest attack on reformist socialism and bourgeois rights discourse. Directly applicable when evaluating Democratic Party compromises and liberal NGO framing in Ohio politics.
Marx · 1875
